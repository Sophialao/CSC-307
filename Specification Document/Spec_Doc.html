  • The system should consist of:

  • All payments to employees should be processed
  • On-time payments considering various types of possible deductions from the paychecks such as loans or leave without payment.

  1. Glossary
  • Commission: percentage of sales, collections, etc., paid to an employee
  • Direct deposit: the electronic transfer of an employee’s net pay directly into financial institution accounts designated by the employee, thus avoiding the need for a paycheck
  • Employee: an individual who performs services for another individual or an organization in return for compensation
  • Net pay: percentage of sales, collections, etc., paid to an employee
  • Gross pay: the total amount received from the employer before any deductions are made
  • Overtime: hours worked by nonexempt employees in excess of maximums set by federal or state law that must be compensated at a premium rate of pay (e.g., under the FLSA, all hours worked over 40 in a workweek must be paid at not less than 150% times the employee’s regular rate of pay)
  • Timesheet:
  • Paid time off: payment received for time not worked due to holiday, illness, vacation, jury duty, bereavement, or the failure of the employer to provide sufficient work
  • Sick pay: replacement wages paid to an employee who cannot work because of an illness or injury that is not work-related

  1. Functional Requirements
  • Keep track of employees’ pay
  • If hourly, keep track of time in/time out
  • Calculated exactly (e.g. 8 hr 43 min is 8 hr 43 min)
  • Overtime is 1.5x regular pay in excess of 8 hours
  • Sick time
  • Commission (rate tbd)
  • Holidays
  • Must be able to approve (by supervisor/manager?)
  • Calculate employee pay once a day
  • Give money if payday
  • Monday every week if hourly
  • Monthly (last working day of month) if salaried
  • Every other Friday if commissioned (for just commission)
  • Payment method must be taken into account
  • Mailed check to employee’s postal address
  • Direct deposit to employee’s bank account
  • Personal loan payment must be taken into account
  • Tax info must be taken into account (state)

  1. Acceptance Criteria
  • The user will able to add new employees
  • New employees will be required to have a last name and email and preferred method of payment(either direct deposit or a check mailed to their home address)
  • There will be optional fields to specify first name, date of birth, gender,and phone number
  • When adding an employee there will be options to specify employee type
  • Either pay by hour or monthly salary with commision specified
  • The user will be able to edit employee information
  • Including name, phone number, address, email, gender
  • The system will track of hours worked by every employee
  • The user can specify any specific employee and see all of their past history along with the total number of hours worked so far that week
  • There will be a check before the hours are submitted to the database
  • The system will be able to calculate the amount to pay to every employee
  • The system will know how to handle both hourly and salary based employees
  • Hourly employees will have their overtime pay automatically applied
